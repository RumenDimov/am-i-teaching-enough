## The 17 Early Learning Goals (ELGs)

Reception year is the final year of the Early Years Foundation Stage (EYFS). By the end of Reception, children are assessed against 17 statutory Early Learning Goals across the 7 EYFS areas.

### Prime Areas (fundamental for learning):
1. **Communication and Language**
   - ELG 1: Listening, Attention and Understanding
   - ELG 2: Speaking

2. **Physical Development**
   - ELG 6: Gross Motor Skills
   - ELG 7: Fine Motor Skills

3. **Personal, Social and Emotional Development**
   - ELG 3: Self-Regulation
   - ELG 4: Managing Self
   - ELG 5: Building Relationships

### Specific Areas (build on prime areas):
4. **Literacy**
   - ELG 8: Comprehension
   - ELG 9: Word Reading
   - ELG 10: Writing

5. **Mathematics**
   - ELG 11: Number
   - ELG 12: Numerical Patterns

6. **Understanding the World**

## Good Level of Development (GLD)

Children are defined as having a **Good Level of Development** at the end of EYFS if they achieve expected level in:
- All ELGs in Communication and Language
- All ELGs in Personal, Social and Emotional Development
- All ELGs in Physical Development
- All ELGs in Literacy
- All ELGs in Mathematics

(Understanding the World and Expressive Arts & Design are assessed but not part of GLD criteria)
